Heat Demand Forecasting Model for Power Plant Operations

(R, Excel, Statistica)

In my role at a company overseeing several power plants in Warsaw, I developed a predictive model for forecasting heat demand as part of an initiative to enhance operational efficiency. The project spanned several critical stages, including data collection, preprocessing, modeling, and implementation. Below are the key aspects of my work:


Data Collection and Preparation:

I gathered extensive datasets, including external temperature forecasts and system input temperatures, covering a 5-year period for model testing.
Using tools like SQL and Excel, I cleaned and processed the data to ensure consistency and quality. Outliers were removed, and missing values were managed to improve model accuracy.

Model Development:

I used statistical software tools, primarily Statistica and R, to build the forecasting model. By leveraging historical temperature data, I created a trendline to understand the relationship between external temperatures and heat demand. A key enhancement I introduced was the inclusion of an additional column in the dataset, which mapped heat demand purely based on temperature. This simple yet effective feature allowed me to visualize the trend and understand temperature-driven demand patterns.
This trendline was later incorporated as an independent variable in the final model, significantly improving the model's accuracy.

Model Validation and Accuracy:

After rigorous testing, the model showed around 50% improvement in predictive accuracy compared to older models used in the system. The updated model accounted for more variables and better captured seasonal variations in heat demand.


Implementation and Impact:

Once validated, the model was integrated into the operational system. The Dispatch Analyst could now use the model's outputs for daily analysis, making more informed and cost-effective decisions based on the forecasted heat demand. The model played a critical role in optimizing resource allocation and improving the overall efficiency of plant operations.

This project significantly contributed to the modernization of our heat demand forecasting system and allowed for more precise and data-driven decision-making within the plant's operational workflow.
